The Story

Seven hundred years,
held in stone

The tower came first. Raised in the fourteenth century as a watchpoint over the valley, its walls were built to outlast the people who laid them — and they did. In the centuries that followed, a farmhouse grew around its base, and the hillside filled with olive trees. Generations pressed their oil here, kept their wine in the cool of the ground floor, and watched the same hills soften into evening.

Then, slowly, the house fell quiet. The presses stopped. Shutters loosened, the roof gave way to the sky, and the olive grove grew wild. For decades it stood empty — a beautiful ruin, remembering.

Its restoration was not a demolition but a conversation. Every original stone that could be saved was saved; the tower, the arched niches, the hand-laid walls remain exactly where they have always stood. Where the old fabric had fallen away, light was invited in — floor-to-ceiling glass, warm oak, quiet modern rooms that frame the landscape rather than compete with it. The result is a home where seven centuries and the present day rest easily in the same walls.

Old walls. New light.
A story still being written.
